Where Leadership, AI,
and Human Development Converge

Dr. Simone Elias is a scholar practitioner, educator, and doctoral faculty member whose work sits at the intersection of leadership development, artificial intelligence, and educational innovation. With over two decades of experience across K to doctoral education, she brings a rare integrated perspective bridging K–12 practice, higher education, and emerging technology ecosystems.

Her research agenda centers on human development as authentic leaders, with a particular focus on life story narratives, critical turning points, and what she conceptualizes as "trigger moments" in leadership formation.

More recently, Dr. Elias has advanced her scholarship into AI governance and policy, addressing questions of accountability, bias, transparency, and human oversight across K–12 and higher education contexts.

As a Senior Dissertation Chair, she mentors scholar practitioners across leadership, equity, and organizational change, guiding doctoral candidates through rigorous applied research on leadership identity and the organizational impact of artificial intelligence.

In addition to her academic work, Dr. Elias has collaborated with Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, and Nuance Communications on projects involving machine learning, natural language systems, and product innovation.

Scholarly Inquiry

Research Agenda

01
Theme One

Leadership Development and Human Formation

Dr. Elias investigates how individuals develop as authentic leaders through lived experience, focusing on life story narratives, critical turning points, and "trigger moments" as catalysts for leadership formation. She examines how identity, context, and meaning making shape leadership across personal and professional domains.

Authentic LeadershipLife Story NarrativesTrigger MomentsIdentity
02
Theme Two

Human and AI Interaction

Extending her leadership scholarship, Dr. Elias explores how artificial intelligence influences leadership identity, decision making, and ethical responsibility. Her work asks how educators and leaders adapt their sense of self and professional practice as technology reshapes their environments and roles.

Human & AI InteractionLeadership IdentityEthical ResponsibilityGenerative AI
03
Theme Three

AI Governance and Policy in Education

Dr. Elias's most recent scholarship addresses how educational systems respond to the growing presence of artificial intelligence, foregrounding accountability, bias, transparency, and human oversight.
